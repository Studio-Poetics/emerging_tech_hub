Understanding Technology Readiness Levels (TRL)

Technology Readiness Levels (TRL) are a systematic metric used to assess the maturity of a particular technology. Originally developed by NASA, the TRL scale ranges from 1 (basic research) to 9 (proven operational capability).

TRL Scale for AI Systems

For AI and machine learning systems, TRL assessment considers factors like data quality, model robustness, system integration, and real-world performance under diverse conditions.

TRL 1-2: Basic Research
TRL 3: Proof of Concept
TRL 4: Lab Validation
TRL 5: Simulated Environment
TRL 6: Prototype Demo
TRL 7: System Integration
TRL 8: System Complete
TRL 9: Proven Operational

Key Insights from the TRL Analysis

The Generality-Readiness Trade-off

Higher TRLs are more achievable for narrow, specialized AI systems. As we move toward more general AI capabilities, the readiness levels decrease significantly.

Industry vs Research Timelines

Industry applications tend to cluster in TRL 6-9, focusing on proven technologies. Research concepts occupy TRL 1-4, exploring next-generation capabilities.

Science Fiction Inspiration

Many concepts from science fiction (AGI, consciousness) remain at TRL 1-2, serving as inspiration for long-term research directions rather than near-term deployments.

What is Experience Mapping?

Experience mapping documents the user's emotional and physical journey through your virtual space. It helps you design intentional experiences by identifying key moments, pain points, and opportunities for engagement.

The 5 Journey Stages:

üö™ Entry

First impression. Where users spawn, what they see immediately. Critical for orientation.

üîç Exploration

Discovery phase. Users navigate, find interesting elements, understand the space structure.

ü§ù Interaction

Engagement moments. Users interact with objects, other users, or content. Peak experience.

‚ö° Decision

Choice points. Users decide where to go next, what to do. Branching paths.

üèÅ Conclusion

Exit or completion. How the experience ends, lasting impression, call-to-action.

How to Map Effectively:

  1. Think Chronologically: Map in order from entry to exit, as users experience it
  2. Be Specific: "User enters gallery" ‚Üí Better: "User spawns facing featured artwork, ambient music plays"
  3. Track Emotions: Note desired feelings (excited, curious) vs. potential issues (confused, overwhelmed)
  4. Identify Friction: Where might users get stuck, lost, or bored? These are optimization opportunities
  5. Design Peaks: Plan 2-3 "wow moments" - high points of engagement or delight
üí° Pro Tip: Map your journey BEFORE building. Use this tool early in design to identify problems and opportunities. Example journey structure:
Entry (Orient) ‚Üí Exploration (Hook) ‚Üí Interaction (Engage) ‚Üí Decision (Branch?) ‚Üí Conclusion (Remember)
üìä After Mapping: Export your journey map as Markdown to share with your team, include in design docs, or use as a testing checklist when building the actual experience.

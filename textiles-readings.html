Fashionable Technology: The Intersection of Design, Fashion, Science and Technology

Sabine Seymour 2008 Springer

Comprehensive guide to wearable technology design covering materials, construction, and creative applications. Essential reading for designers entering fashion tech.

Key Topics:

  • Design Thinking: Applying design methodology to wearable technology
  • Material Innovation: Smart fabrics and responsive materials
  • Body-Centered Design: Considering movement, comfort, and human factors
  • Case Studies: Pioneering projects in fashion technology

For Designers:

Framework for thinking about technology as fashion material and designing wearables that people actually want to wear.

Wearable Computing: A First Step Toward Personal Imaging

Steve Mann 1997 IEEE Computer

Foundational paper from wearable computing pioneer Steve Mann exploring principles of body-worn technology and its implications.

Key Concepts:

  • Always-On: Persistent access to computation and information
  • Context Awareness: Technology that responds to environment and activity
  • Augmentation: Enhancing human capabilities through wearables
  • Design Principles: Considerations for body-worn devices

For Product Managers:

Historical perspective on wearable computing that informs current design decisions.

Designing for Wearability

Gemperle et al. 1998 IEEE ISWC

Research identifying key factors for comfortable and practical wearable devices through body mapping and user studies.

Design Guidelines:

  • Body Placement: Optimal locations for different types of wearables
  • Form Language: Shapes that work with body contours
  • Attachment Methods: Strategies for keeping devices secure and comfortable
  • Movement: Designing for dynamic body motion

For Designers:

Practical guidelines for making wearables that people can actually live with.

Practical guide exploring the intersection of fashion design and technology, with focus on creative applications and designer workflows.

Key Areas:

  • Material Selection: Choosing smart textiles for design goals
  • Prototyping: Methods for testing wearable concepts
  • Fabrication: Construction techniques for electronic textiles
  • Industry Trends: Commercial applications and market insights

For Designers:

Bridges fashion design and technology with practical, maker-friendly approaches.

Wearable Technology and the Body: Designing for Wearability

Tromp & Nachtigall 2015 Design Issues

Design research examining how wearables become part of personal identity and daily life, with framework for body-centered design.

Design Considerations:

  • Body Integration: Designing technology that feels natural to wear

  • Personal Expression: Wearables as extensions of identity
  • Long-term Use: Designing for sustained adoption

How to Get What You Want: A Practical Guide to E-Textile Materials

The essential e-textile encyclopedia. Comprehensive documentation of materials, techniques, and construction methods from leading practitioners.

Survey of e-textile materials, fabrication techniques, and design approaches with focus on creative applications and maker methods.

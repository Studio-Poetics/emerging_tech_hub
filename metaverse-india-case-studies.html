Indian Metaverse Case Studies

How Indian Brands Pioneered Virtual Commerce & Engagement (2022-2024)

Market Context: India's Metaverse Adoption

Key Statistics

  • $800 Billion - Projected global metaverse market by 2024 (Bloomberg)
  • 500,000+ Indian users showed interest in NFTs and metaverse projects (November 2021)
  • #5 Globally - India's ranking in metaverse project interest
  • 2022-2023 - Peak period for Indian brand metaverse experiments

Between 2022 and 2023, Indian brands across retail, entertainment, and sports sectors began experimenting with metaverse technology. While the market was nascent, major players like Flipkart, Tanishq, and IPL teams launched pioneering initiatives to test virtual engagement and commerce.

This wave of experimentation offers valuable lessons about what worked, what failed, and the gap between marketing hype and sustainable utility.

Case Study 1: Flipkart Flipverse

E-Commerce Giant's Gamified Virtual Shopping Experience

Background & Launch

In 2022, Flipkart launched Flipkart Labs to investigate non-fungible tokens (NFTs), digital immersive shops, and blockchain-linked use cases. This culminated in two major metaverse initiatives:

FireDrops Platform

NFT platform launched earlier in 2022, allowing partner brands to create digital collectibles and engage with customers through blockchain-backed loyalty programs.

Flipverse Launch

Virtual shopping experience launched October 17, 2022, on Android app for Diwali festival sales in partnership with eDAO (Polygon-incubated firm).

Technical Implementation

Technology Stack

  • Blockchain: Built on Polygon (Ethereum Layer-2 solution)
  • Platform: Integrated into existing Flipkart Android app
  • Partner: eDAO for metaverse development
  • Format: Gamified, interactive virtual brand theaters

Brand Partnerships & Experiences

Multiple brands set up "experience theaters" in Flipverse during the Diwali 2022 launch:

Participating Brands

Puma, Noise, Nivea, Lavie, Tokyo Talkies, Campus, VIP, Ajmal Perfumes, and Himalaya created branded virtual spaces where users could:

  • Collect Flipkart Supercoins (loyalty points)
  • Earn digital collectibles
  • Unlock exclusive deals
  • Participate in gamified treasure hunts

Big Billion Days 2023 Evolution

For the Big Billion Days 2023 festival sale, Flipkart evolved the Flipverse concept with a metaverse treasure hunt:

  • Users navigated virtual stores to unlock discounts
  • Gamified discovery of deals and offers
  • Result: 15% increase in app downloads during the campaign

Strategic Partnership: Polygon Blockchain Center of Excellence

On December 2, 2022, Flipkart formalized its blockchain commitment by partnering with Polygon to establish a Centre of Excellence (CoE) for Web3 and metaverse commerce research.

Partnership Goals

"This partnership will pioneer research and development at the intersection of Web3 and experiential retail... with the aim to bring the next billion users to Web3."

— Sandeep Nailwal, Co-Founder, Polygon

Business Model & Monetization

  • Customer Acquisition: Gamification to drive app downloads and engagement
  • Brand Partnerships: Revenue from brands creating virtual experiences
  • Loyalty Integration: Supercoins ecosystem connecting physical and virtual commerce
  • Data Insights: Understanding customer behavior in immersive environments

Lessons & Outcomes

What Worked

  • Integration into existing app (low friction)
  • Festival timing (Diwali traffic spike)
  • Gamification increased engagement
  • Strong brand partnerships

Challenges

  • Limited to Android initially
  • Short-lived campaign-based activation
  • Unclear long-term value proposition
  • High development costs for limited ROI

Case Study 2: Tanishq Rivaahverse

India's First Metaverse Jewelry Collection Launch

Background & Innovation

Rivaah by Tanishq, the wedding jewellery-focused sub-brand from India's leading jewelry retailer, became the first Indian jewellery brand to launch a collection through an experiential event in the metaverse.

Launch Event: April 7, 2022

Rivaah unveiled its 'Romance of Polki' collection through a virtual press conference in the "Rivaahverse" - a custom metaverse experience designed specifically for the launch.

Technical Implementation

Platform Partner

Queppelin provided the metaverse infrastructure using their GatherInVR platform to facilitate the virtual press conference.

Agency Partner

Adfactor PR conceptualized the metaverse press conference strategy and coordinated the event execution.

Experience Features

1. Avatar Creation & Social Interaction

  • Guests created personalized 3D avatars
  • Virtual networking and conversation with other attendees
  • Live interaction during the collection unveiling

2. 360° Jewelry Viewing

  • 14 signature pieces from the Romance of Polki collection displayed
  • Detailed examination from multiple angles
  • Zoom capabilities to inspect craftsmanship details
  • Virtual appreciation of Rajasthani-inspired designs and artisanal work

3. AR Try-On Integration

Guests could try on the Showstopper piece using QR codes that triggered augmented reality experiences on their mobile phones - bridging virtual viewing with personalized try-on.

The 'Romance of Polki' Collection

The collection launched in the metaverse was:

  • Theme: Celebration of time-honoured artisanal craftsmanship
  • Inspiration: The land of Rajasthan
  • Technique: Traditional Polki jewelry (uncut diamonds set in gold)
  • Target: Indian wedding jewelry market

Strategic Rationale

Why Metaverse for Jewelry?

  • Visual Appreciation: Jewelry relies heavily on visual details - metaverse allows detailed 360° viewing impossible in physical press conferences
  • Novelty Factor: First-mover advantage in Indian jewelry sector
  • Wedding Market: Indian weddings are increasingly digital-savvy; couples research online before purchasing
  • Press Coverage: Innovative launch format generated significant media attention
  • Cultural Fit: Virtual try-on and exploration aligns with modern Indian bride's shopping journey

Outcomes & Impact

Successes

  • Massive PR coverage as India's first metaverse jewelry launch
  • Successfully demonstrated 3D visualization value for jewelry
  • Integrated AR try-on bridged virtual-physical gap
  • Positioned Tanishq as innovation leader

Limitations

  • One-time event, not sustained platform
  • Limited to press/influencer audience
  • Unclear consumer adoption beyond novelty
  • No ongoing Rivaahverse for customer engagement

Other Indian Metaverse Initiatives (2022-2023)

Sports & Entertainment

Gujarat Titans (IPL)

Launch: Team logo and "The Titans' Dugout" virtual space on Spatial.io platform.

Purpose: Fan engagement and brand presence in virtual worlds during IPL season.

Chennai Super Kings (IPL)

Initiative: Virtual stadiums on Decentraland with cricket mini-games and live-streamed match viewing.

Impact: 2024 virtual fan zone attracted 8 million users, increasing merchandise sales by 25%.

ALTBalaji - Lock Upp

Format: Metaverse tokens for reality show allowing viewers to interact with contestants and own collectible cards.

Innovation: First Indian OTT platform to tokenize reality TV experience.

Brand Marketing Activations

ITC Fabelle Chocolates

Event: Sponsored India's first metaverse wedding on YUG platform (February 2022) alongside Coca-Cola India.

Experience:

  • Groom gifted bride metaverse version of Fabelle Trinity Truffles
  • Virtual Fabelle Chocolates cart for guests
  • Physical chocolate delivery to guests' homes from virtual selection

Significance: Demonstrated phygital (physical + digital) commerce model

Cadbury Dairy Milk Silk

Campaign: Valentine's Day 2022 AR experience with secret messages

Features:

  • Real-time personalization of AR overlays on Cadbury ads
  • Immersive 360-degree virtual environment
  • Message unlocking and sharing mechanism

E-Commerce Platforms

Myntra (Flipkart subsidiary) actively explored metaverse shopping and augmented reality to personalize experiences, utilizing immersive storytelling, haptic technologies, and gamification for diverse consumer engagement.

Critical Analysis: Hype vs. Reality

⚠️ The FOMO Trap

Warning: Many Indian "metaverses" were short-lived marketing stunts driven by FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) during the 2022 Web3 hype cycle. True utility requires persistence and interoperability, not just one-time 3D marketing events.

What Worked: Sustainable Patterns

Integration Over Isolation

Flipkart's integration into existing app worked better than standalone metaverse platforms that required new downloads and onboarding.

Gamification & Rewards

Loyalty points, discounts, and collectibles drove real engagement - not the "metaverse" label itself.

Phygital Bridge

ITC Fabelle's physical chocolate delivery from virtual cart showed promise for connecting virtual selection with physical fulfillment.

What Failed: Common Pitfalls

One-Time Events

Tanishq Rivaahverse was a brilliant press event but didn't create ongoing customer value. No sustained Rivaahverse exists for regular engagement.

Empty Virtual Malls

Many brands created virtual spaces that lacked compelling reasons to return after initial curiosity wore off.

Platform Fragmentation

Brands scattered across Decentraland, Spatial.io, custom platforms - no interoperability or persistent identity for users.

Blockchain Overhead

Polygon partnerships sounded impressive but added complexity most consumers neither understood nor valued.

Lessons for the Future

  1. Utility First, Buzzwords Second: Focus on solving real customer problems (better visualization, convenience, personalization) rather than chasing "metaverse" trends.
  2. Sustainable Engagement: One-time campaigns don't build metaverse ecosystems. Consider long-term retention strategies.
  3. Interoperability Matters: Customers won't maintain separate avatars, wallets, and profiles for each brand's isolated metaverse.
  4. Mobile-First Reality: In India's mobile-dominated market, metaverse experiences must work seamlessly on smartphones, not just high-end VR headsets.
  5. Cultural Relevance: Tanishq's wedding-focused approach and Fabelle's cultural moments show promise - align virtual experiences with Indian consumer contexts.

The 2024 Reality Check

As of 2024, most Indian brand metaverse initiatives from 2022 have quietly disappeared. Flipkart continues blockchain/Web3 research through its CoE, but Flipverse is not actively marketed. Tanishq returned to traditional digital marketing. IPL virtual stadiums see sporadic activity.

Verdict: The 2022-2023 wave was experimental and educational. The brands that survived were those that integrated metaverse elements as features, not standalone destinations.

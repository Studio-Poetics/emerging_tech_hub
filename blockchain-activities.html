üß† Strategic Thought Experiments

üîë The Digital Identity Paradox

High Complexity

The Question:

If blockchain enables truly self-sovereign identity, how do we balance individual privacy with society's need for accountability and safety?

Explore:

  • Can anonymous transactions coexist with tax collection?
  • How do we prevent crime while preserving privacy?
  • Who decides what information should be permanently recorded?
  • What happens when immutable records become inconvenient?

Real-World Applications:

Digital Voting Financial Privacy Social Credit Systems Reputation Networks

‚öñÔ∏è Blockchain and Economic Inequality

High Complexity

The Question:

Does blockchain technology democratize finance and reduce inequality, or does it amplify existing advantages and create new forms of digital divide?

Consider:

  • Who benefits from "permissionless" systems that require technical knowledge?
  • How do transaction fees affect accessibility for the global poor?
  • Does "code is law" favor those who can write or influence the code?
  • Can decentralized systems address systemic inequality?

Multiple Perspectives:

Global South Unbanked Populations Tech Elite Traditional Finance

üèõÔ∏è Central Bank Digital Currency Dominance (2028)

Probable

Major economies launch CBDCs that become the primary medium of exchange, while permissionless cryptocurrencies exist in a parallel economy with limited integration.

Strategic Implications:

  • Monetary Policy: Real-time economic control and surveillance
  • Privacy: Tension between convenience and financial privacy
  • Innovation: Regulatory frameworks determine innovation pace
  • Geopolitics: Currency competition intensifies between nations

Strategic Questions:

  • How do businesses operate across CBDC and crypto economies?
  • What role do stablecoins play in this scenario?
  • How do you preserve financial privacy and freedom?
  • What new business models emerge from programmable money?

üåü Post-Scarcity Economics (2040)

Speculative

Advanced automation and AI make most goods abundant and cheap, while blockchain enables new forms of value creation around attention, creativity, and social coordination.

Economic Paradigm Shifts:

  • Value Creation: Shifts from production to curation and coordination
  • Work Definition: Human activity focuses on creativity and social connection
  • Ownership Models: Shared abundance vs. individual accumulation
  • Governance: Managing abundance requires new coordination mechanisms

Questions to Explore:

  • What becomes scarce when everything else is abundant?
  • How do you incentivize contribution in a post-scarcity world?
  • What role does blockchain play in resource allocation?
  • How do we prevent dystopian concentration of automated production?
